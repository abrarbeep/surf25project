Abstract

This research continued efforts to modernize the construction permitting process in Southeast Texas by exploring the feasibility of an AI-driven, digital permitting platform. During summer 2025, we developed a foundational system design, conducted stakeholder outreach, and built a project website.

Project Overview & Rationale

Permitting delays in Southeast Texas hinder development and inflate costs for developers and governments alike. Each municipality in the Beaumont-Port Arthur MSA has unique codes and workflows, making it difficult for applicants to navigate the process.

Our goal was to design an AI-powered permitting system that would streamline permitting through:

  • Automated compliance checks
  • Predictive approval timelines
  • Real-time tracking
  • Mobile/web app interface

Economic Foundation

These improvements align with Paul Romer's endogenous growth theory, which states that technological advancements can directly drive regional economic growth by improving productivity.

Key Findings & Insights

Systemic Challenges

Permitting inefficiencies extend beyond SETX. Delays arise from unclear requirements, manual reviews, and lack of transparency, causing months-long project setbacks.

Contractor Outreach Complexity

Extremely low response rates and legal constraints highlighted the need for alternative engagement strategies like in-person focus groups.

8% Response Rate (2/25)

Economic Impact Potential

By reducing permitting inefficiencies, our system could support faster project starts and stimulate local economic growth through quicker capital deployment.
